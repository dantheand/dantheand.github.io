About Me

I am an avid sailor, researcher, and educator. After delivering sailboats for a year and a half in the Pacific, I re-entered academia through the Santa Barbara City College Biological Sciences program. At SBCC, I tutored several courses and took part in an research internship as a Maxwell Scholar.

In the fall of 2014, I transferred to the Molecular & Cell Biology Program at the University of California, Berkeley. I am currently an undergraduate tutor for the UC Berkeley Biophysical Chemistry course and conduct research in the lab of Professor Adam Arkin. During the summer of 2015, I was an Amgen Scholar in the lab of Professor Jacquin Niles at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.

Throughout my higher education I have been dedicated to communicating science. I have held teaching positions in all but two semesters of college and continually integrate technology into my teaching endeavors. At SBCC, I programmed a web application to help students learn taxonomy. At UC Berkeley, I have been developing a set of video lectures to complement the Biophysical Chemistry course material. And, most recently, I act as a teaching assistant for the MITx Principles of Synthetic Biology course on edX.

I am now seeking to enter a doctoral program so that I may contribute to creating highly-engineerable biological systems through synthetic biology research.
